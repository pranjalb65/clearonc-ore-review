Line of therapy and treatment plan

enhancement found

Line: Line 1 (trastuzumab + paclitaxel), derived from the medication record. Treatment-naive prior to this.

Recommended plan (generated, cited): for node-positive (N1), HER2-positive, HR-positive Stage IIB disease:

  • Neoadjuvant dual HER2 blockade plus a taxane (trastuzumab + pertuzumab + taxane, e.g. THP or TCHP), then surgery. Cited: NeoSphere, PMID 22153890; Perjeta FDA neoadjuvant indication (tumor >2 cm or node-positive).
  • Response-adapted adjuvant therapy: residual invasive disease at surgery to adjuvant T-DM1 for 14 cycles (KATHERINE, PMID 30516102; OS update, PMID 39813643); pathologic complete response to complete one year of HER2 therapy (trastuzumab + pertuzumab, APHINITY, PMID 28581356).
  • Adjuvant endocrine therapy for the HR-positive component, an aromatase inhibitor for at least 5 years, after chemotherapy and concurrent with continued HER2 therapy.
Key finding for the second opinion: the recorded regimen (single-agent trastuzumab + paclitaxel) is the regimen validated for node-negative small tumors (APT, PMID 25564897). This patient is node-positive (N1), where dual HER2 blockade by adding pertuzumab has the clearest benefit. ORE recommends adding pertuzumab and planning response-adapted adjuvant therapy.

Pharmacogenomics (PGx)

conditional

DPYD: untested. Not required for the current trastuzumab + paclitaxel regimen (no fluoropyrimidine). It becomes relevant only if capecitabine is later considered (for example as an alternative in residual disease, or a later line).

If capecitabine is planned, test DPYD first: CPIC says poor metabolizers (activity score 0 or 0.5) should avoid fluoropyrimidines. CPIC DPYD guideline, DOI 10.1002/cpt.911 (PMID 29152729); FDA capecitabine and 5-FU labels now flag DPD deficiency.
